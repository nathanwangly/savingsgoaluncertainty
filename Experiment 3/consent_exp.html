Participant information statement and consent form

FINANCIAL DECISION-MAKING: SPENDING AND SAVING BEHAVIOUR

  1. What is the research study about?

    You are invited to participate in a study of financial decision-making. We hope to learn how people make financial decisions in uncertain situations. You were selected as a possible participant in this study because of your involvement in Psychology 1.

  2. Do I have to take part in this research study?

    Participation in this research study is voluntary. If you do not want to take part, you do not have to. If you decide to take part and later change your mind, you are free to withdraw from the study at any stage.

  3. What does participation in this research require, and are there any risks involved?

    If you decide to participate, you will complete a computer-based task which will take approximately 30 minutes. No risks are reasonably expected as a result of your participation in this study.

  4. Recompense to participants

    As a participant of the Psychology 1 research pool, you will receive course credit for your time spent here (0.5 credits for the 30-minute duration). In addition, you may win a monetary amount depending on your performance in the computer-based task. The highest expected monetary return for participating in this experiment is $20.

  5. What will happen to information about me?

    The information that you give us will be kept for seven years after the projectâ€™s completion. We will store information about you in a non-identifiable format at UNSW (Kensington Campus).

  6. What if I want to withdraw from the research study?

    Your decision whether or not to participate will not prejudice your future relations with The University of New South Wales. If you decide to participate, you are free to withdraw your consent and to discontinue participation at any time without prejudice.

  7. What should I do if I have further questions about my involvement in the research study?

    If you have any questions or concerns following your participation, Nathan Wang-Ly (nathan.wang-ly@unsw.edu.au) and his supervisor, Professor Ben Newell (ben.newell@unsw.edu.au) will be happy to address them.

  8. What if I have a complaint or any concerns about the research study?

    If you have a complaint regarding any aspect of the study or the way it is being conducted, please contact the UNSW Human Ethics Coordinator:
